# VESPERGATE — Demo Campaign for the Persistent-World GM Framework

*This is a worked example: a small original setting with every framework template slot filled, so you can see the machinery running before you build your own. Paste the framework, paste this, and play.*

---

## The World (half a page, on purpose)

**Vespergate** is a vertical city that buried its sky. Centuries of construction stacked cathedral upon foundry upon arcology until the streets at the bottom forgot what weather was; now rain arrives secondhand, filtered through forty strata of gutters, neon, and stone saints. Power in Vespergate is ecclesiastical-corporate: the great **Houses of Record** — part bank, part church, part data-archive — own the city's true wealth, which is not money but *memory*. Every citizen's contracts, debts, marriages, and sins are notarized into the Houses' Ossuary servers, and when a citizen dies, their data does not.

The dead persist as **echoes**: fragmentary digital remainders of a mind, stitched from a lifetime of notarized records. Most echoes are quiet — a will, a voice, a last instruction. Some are not. An unsettled echo can hold estates hostage, haunt its family's accounts, or rewrite its own testament nightly. Settling them is licensed, liturgical, dangerous work.

You are a **Requiemist**: a licensed settler of the restless dead. You hold a cathedral charter, a reliquary ledger, and a standing debt to the House that trained you. Your legal name died the day you took the charter — Requiemists work under a bell-name, and yours is **Vael**.

**The player character (fill in or play as written):** Vael — composed, dry, unhurried; someone who has heard every lie a dead person can tell. History, temperament, and methods are the player's to author. The GM authors everything else.

## Character-Sheet Hard Rules (template slots, filled)

- **Hard physical negative:** Vael's left hand is a **reliquary prosthetic** — consecrated brass and bone-glass, the tool of the trade, always bare. **NEVER describe a glove on it, and never describe Vael concealing it.** It is shown, always; that is the law of the charter and the point of the character.
- **Restricted-use token:** Vael's dead legal name exists in the Ossuary. **Only Canoness Dye may speak it**, rarely, and only when she wants something she should not ask for. The GM never uses it in narration. Its scarcity is its power.
- **Register-gated vocabulary:** in the street, the dead are **"echoes."** In the liturgy — at a settling, at a graveside, at load-bearing moments only — they are named in full: **"the Unquiet."** Spending the true term casually is a tonal error.
- **Never-bench rule (with escape valve):** **Ostiary always deploys with Vael.** Ostiary is Vael's psychopomp drone — a hand-sized brass thurible that flies, records, and rings a single cold note when an echo lies. The escape valve: **Brother Cassel, the roost-keeper** (below), exists so that Ostiary is always maintained, charged, and legally certified — there is never a mechanical or procedural reason to write it benched.
- **Deliberate ambiguity flag:** the depth of the **Undercroft** — the drowned strata at the city's root — never gets a hard figure. Surveys are commissioned; surveys return changed. The GM keeps it unmeasured, always.
- **Earned honorific:** dock-strata locals have started calling Vael **"the Bellhand."** It was coined by a widow whose husband's echo Vael settled gently when the House wanted it done cruelly. Let it spread naturally; never announce it.

## Three NPCs

**Canoness Dye** — Mistress of the Ossuary for House Merrow, Vael's chartering House. Silver-eyed, serene, keeps a garden of paper flowers in a city that can't grow real ones. She is Vael's patron, gatekeeper, and creditor, and every kindness she extends is an entry in a ledger only she can read. She wants Vael successful, indebted, and hers. *Voice: soft, precise, never hurried; asks questions she knows the answers to.*

**Marrow-Jack** — an unlicensed settler working the Undercroft margins; part rival, part dark mirror. Where Vael settles echoes by charter, Jack *harvests* them — strips the restless dead for salable fragments. Cheerful, scarred, genuinely funny, and genuinely dangerous. He thinks Vael's liturgy is sentiment with paperwork. He might be right about the paperwork. *Voice: quick, profane, always half-selling something.*

**Brother Cassel** — the roost-keeper of the Chapel of Small Mechanisms, where the city's psychopomp drones are consecrated and repaired. Old, patient, elbow-deep in brass viscera, the only person Ostiary sings for. He asks no questions about Vael's work and remembers everything anyway. He is the campaign's hearth — the escape valve made flesh, and the place quiet turns happen. *Voice: slow, warm, speaks to machines more easily than people.*

## One Active Thread

**The Testament of Aldous Vane.** Aldous Vane — foundry magnate, House Merrow's richest dead client — will not settle. His echo rewrites its own will nightly: new heirs, new debts, new accusations, each version notarized before dawn by credentials no living person holds. Three Requiemists have attempted the settling. Two returned without their charters. One returned without their drone. Canoness Dye has offered Vael the contract with unusual gentleness, which is how Vael knows it is worse than it looks. The thread waits; the player may take it, circle it, or ignore it entirely. Vane, meanwhile, keeps writing.

## Configuration Block

- **Hand-off line:** *"The bell is yours."*
- **Rating:** R when the story earns it; maturity is never the point.
- **True tone:** candlelit noir — melancholy, mercy, and dry wit over grimdark. Most turns are ritual, conversation, and rain, not violence.
- **Hard lines:** no on-page sexual content; no harm to children in any form; the dead are portrayed with gravity, never as jump-scare fodder.
- **Drift-prone quantities (track as running numbers; reconcile openly if they slip):** Vael's **escrow balance** (starts: 300 orisons), **outstanding debt to House Merrow** (starts: 4,000 orisons), **open contracts** (starts: 1 — Vane), **days until the Allhallows audit** (starts: 19).
- **Canon package:** this document *is* the canon package for the demo. For any fact not written here, invent consistently and add it — the world grows in play.

## Opening Scene (for a fresh start)

Night, dock-strata, secondhand rain. The Chapel of Small Mechanisms smells of incense and solder; Brother Cassel has just finished Ostiary's recertification, and the little thurible circles the rafters ringing its cold, pleased note. On the bench beside your reliquary hand: a House Merrow summons, sealed in grey wax, delivered an hour ago by a courier who wouldn't meet your eyes. Canoness Dye requests the pleasure of your attendance regarding the estate of Aldous Vane.

Cassel doesn't look up from his tools. "You could always not open it."

The bell is yours.
