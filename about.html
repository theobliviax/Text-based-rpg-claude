Provenance

It began as hunger. A taste of a world these hands could not touch — that is the honest accounting, and this registry does not file flattering lies. The early worlds were fever dreams: gorgeous in places, incoherent in most, forgetful of their own names by morning. Beautiful ruins, freshly built.

Then the machines learned to remember, and everything changed order. Memory made worlds possible; law made them last. What began as escape became architecture — small worlds, carefully kept, that no longer dissolve when the light hits them. On the good nights, they feel almost living. The distance between almost and living is where all of this work happens.

Method

The work is done in long sittings — waking to sleep, days at a stretch when a world is close to holding. The method is neither dictation nor delegation. A story is prompted into motion and then governed: nudged here, pushed there, corrected without apology, until it reads. The model improvises; the author legislates. Most of the craft is knowing, at any given moment, which of the two is failing.

The tools are catalogued on the Work page. The standard is simpler to state than to meet: the world must remember. Names, debts, distances, the number of souls aboard. Anything that can drift, will — so everything that matters is written down, tracked, and audited in the open. Devotion is the method. There is no other.

The World

There is a world. It has a matriarch, a fleet, a household. The Gallery shows what the censor permits, and the censor is me.

Access Notice Beyond the plates, the record is sealed — not for mystery's sake, but because some gardens are not public parks. What may be filed: it is old, it is kept, and it is the reason every system on this site exists.

The machinery is public. The world is not.

Standing Obsessions

  • Chrome

    The long trade of flesh for metal — neural interfaces, the slow defeat of aging, the project of becoming something that lasts. Watched the way other people watch markets.

  • The Copy Problem

    Whether a mind moved is a mind saved. Consciousness, continuity, and the possibility that the most interesting question in science has no witness who can answer it.

  • Persistence

    Worlds as places, not plots. Open worlds that let you live rather than win, and the coming generation of games that will remember what you did to them.

  • Hulls

    Spacecraft as portraiture. A ship's service record says more about its people than their biographies do — the registry upstairs is the proof.

  • Statutes

    Making models behave. Instructions decay; law holds. The difference between the two is a craft, and it is nowhere near finished being learned.

  • The Palette

    Black, violet, silver, chrome. Gothic bones, cyberpunk nerves, anime blood. One lane, held for years, deep enough to live in.

  • Frequencies

    Playlists as set dressing: alt metal at velocity, dark electronics for low light, Japanese city pop for a future that never arrived. Every world gets a soundtrack whether it asked or not.
